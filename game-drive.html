About This Activity

The Heart of Every East Africa Safari

The game drive is the definitive East Africa experience — a private open-roof 4WD at dawn or dusk, a born-and-raised local naturalist at the wheel, and a landscape that has remained unchanged for millennia.

Unlike almost any other wildlife experience on earth, the East African game drive places you at eye level with the Big Five in their natural, undisturbed habitat. No fences. No feeding. No schedules imposed on the animals. Just you, your guide, and the wild world as it has always been.

Our game drives are all private — your vehicle, your guide, your pace. We stop when you want to stop, stay with a pride of lions as long as you wish, and position for photographs without compromise. Every drive is tailored to your interests, whether that's predator tracking, birdwatching, photography or simply absorbing the quiet enormity of the African bush.

Hour by Hour

What To Expect on a Game Drive

Your guide arrives at your tent or room with a thermos of hot coffee, Kenyan chai or local herbal tea before first light. There's a reason we start this early — the bush comes alive at dawn in a way the afternoon never replicates. Lions return from the night hunt, cheetahs scan the open plains from termite mounds, and the golden light begins to paint the savanna.

Your open-roof 4WD is loaded with cold water, snacks and all equipment. Your guide briefs you on the area, recent sightings from other guides in the network, and what to watch for today.

Guide pick-up at your accommodation — punctual, every time
Hot drinks and light snacks served before departure
Morning briefing — recent sightings, animal behaviour, route plan
Enter the reserve at first light through your designated gate

The first hour after sunrise is the most electric time in the bush. Predators are still active from the night — a pride of lions may be finishing a kill, a cheetah scanning for prey, or a leopard dragging its meal into a fig tree as the light turns golden. Your guide uses radio contact with the wider guide network to locate the most exciting current sightings.

The vehicle moves slowly and deliberately, your guide reading tracks, dung and vegetation to anticipate where animals will be before you see them. This is the skill that separates a great guide from a good one.

Primary predator tracking — lions, cheetah and leopard zones
Radio network coordination with fellow guides for live sighting updates
Elephant family groups often moving in the early morning coolness
Bird activity at its peak — over 500 species possible in a single morning

Your guide selects a scenic viewpoint — a riverbank, a kopje (rock outcrop), or a tree-shaded clearing — and lays out a proper full breakfast from the vehicle's storage. Scrambled eggs, toast, fruit, yoghurt, juice and more, eaten with the sounds of the bush all around you. No restaurant in the world serves a view like this.

This is also a chance to stretch your legs briefly, use the bush facilities, and discuss what you've seen so far with your guide — who is always happy to explain behaviour, answer questions and share stories.

Full hot or cold breakfast set up in the reserve at a scenic location
Time to stretch, photograph the scenery and debrief with your guide
Tea, coffee and juice refreshments throughout
Bush Breakfast included

As the predators rest in the midday warmth, the vast herds of East Africa take centre stage. Elephant family groups move between water sources with incredible deliberateness, giraffe crane impossibly elegant necks into acacia crowns, and zebra and wildebeest drift across the open plains in their thousands.

Your guide navigates to waterholes, river crossings and grazing grounds, positioning the vehicle with expertise for photographs and extended observation. During migration season (Jul–Oct), this section of the drive focuses on the Mara River zones where the crossings are most likely to happen.

Waterhole stake-outs — multiple species congregating at single water points
Elephant family herd tracking across their morning range
Jul–Oct: Mara River crossing zones — wildebeest migration
Hippo pools and crocodile sightings along river margins

For half-day drives, your guide returns you to camp or lodge by midday — in time for lunch, a rest and the afternoon pool. For full-day drives, we set up a proper bush picnic lunch in the reserve and continue into the afternoon. Full-day drives cover significantly more ground and consistently produce the most memorable encounters.

The choice between half-day and full-day is entirely yours and can be decided on the day based on how you're feeling and what the morning has produced.

Half-day: return to camp/lodge by 12:30 PM
Full-day: bush picnic lunch served in the reserve
Full-day: afternoon drive 2:00 PM – 6:30 PM through golden hour
Sundowner drinks at a scenic viewpoint to close the day

Wear neutral or earth-toned clothing (khaki, beige, olive, grey) — avoid bright colours and white which can unsettle wildlife. Mornings are cool, so bring a warm layer for early starts. Game drives are very safe when guided by experienced professionals — your guide knows exactly how to behave around wildlife and never places guests in any risk. Your guide follows all park safety regulations strictly.
