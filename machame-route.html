About This Trek

Why Choose the Machame Route?

Known as the "Whiskey Route" for its challenging and rewarding character, the Machame Route is Kilimanjaro's most scenic and most popular path — for very good reason.

The route begins in the lush rainforest on the southern slopes, climbing steadily through heath and moorland before reaching the spectacular Shira Plateau. The iconic traverse from Lava Tower down to the Barranco Valley is one of Africa's finest high-altitude walks — and the Barranco Wall ascent on Day 4 is a highlight few trekkers ever forget.

With an excellent "walk high, sleep low" acclimatisation profile, the 8-day version of the Machame Route gives you an extra summit day and a significantly higher success rate than the 7-day version. Our guides strongly recommend the longer option for all fitness levels.

Day-by-Day Programme

8-Day Machame Route Itinerary

After an early morning transfer from Moshi, you arrive at Machame Gate (1,830m) for registration and a safety briefing from your lead guide. The trek begins through a glorious montane rainforest — dense canopy, rushing streams and colourful birdlife accompanying you all the way up.

After approximately 5–6 hours of steady climbing you reach Machame Camp at 3,000m. Your mountain cook has hot soup and dinner waiting. Your guide conducts the first pulse-oximeter reading and settles you in for your first night on the mountain.

Transfer from Moshi hotel to Machame Gate (1,830m)
Registration, kit check and safety briefing
Trek through montane rainforest to Machame Camp (3,000m)
Hot dinner and first pulse-oximeter health check
Lunch (packed) Dinner Camp: Machame (3,000m)

A hot breakfast and you're off. Today's ascent leaves the rainforest behind and climbs through heath and moorland — giant heather trees, lobelias and groundsel plants that look like something from another planet. The trail narrows to a ridge with spectacular views back over the forest canopy.

You reach the broad, flat Shira Plateau at 3,840m — one of the world's highest plateaux and a remnant of Kilimanjaro's original volcanic cone. The open expanse allows stunning panoramic views of the summit above and the plains of Tanzania below.

Morning health check + breakfast at Machame Camp
Ridge walk through heathland with panoramic views
Arrival at Shira Plateau and Shira 2 Camp (3,840m)
Optional short acclimatisation walk above camp (4,000m)
Breakfast Lunch (packed) Dinner

The most important acclimatisation day — and one of the most scenic. You climb to the volcanic Lava Tower at 4,630m, where you pause for lunch and let your body begin adapting to the altitude before descending into the dramatic Barranco Valley.

This "walk high, sleep low" technique is the key to the Machame Route's high summit success rate — reaching 4,630m accelerates red blood cell production without the risks of sleeping at that altitude. The descent into the valley reveals giant Senecio groundsel trees that can only be described as prehistoric.

Traverse east across the Shira Plateau towards Kibo
Climb to Lava Tower (4,630m) — lunch with summit views
Descent through the Barranco Valley to Barranco Camp (3,950m)
Evening health check — altitude sickness monitoring
Breakfast Lunch (Lava Tower) Dinner

The day opens with the Barranco Wall — a spectacular 300m near-vertical rock face that trekkers scramble up using hands and feet. Despite its dramatic appearance, no ropes or technical equipment are required, and our guides have done this hundreds of times. It is one of the most exhilarating highlights of any Kilimanjaro experience.

Once at the top, the trail continues along a series of ridges and valleys to Karanga Camp. The relatively short day (4–5 hours) allows time to rest, hydrate and prepare for the longer days ahead.

Early start — scramble up the iconic Barranco Wall (300m)
Ridge walking with dramatic Kibo summit views
Arrival at Karanga Camp (4,035m) for an early afternoon rest
Health checks and summit briefing from your guide
Breakfast Lunch (packed) Dinner

A shorter, steep climb to Barafu Camp at 4,673m — your basecamp for the summit attempt. The name means "ice" in Swahili, and the camp sits on a barren volcanic ridge with dramatic 360° views. The vegetation is sparse, the air is noticeably thinner, and the atmosphere is electric.

After lunch and a compulsory rest period, your guide conducts a full summit briefing — covering the route, turnaround times, pacing strategy, and what to expect during the night ascent. An early dinner at 5:30 PM is followed by lights-out at 7:00 PM. Wake-up call is midnight.

Morning climb from Karanga to Barafu Camp (4,673m)
Mandatory rest period — hydration and nutrition focus
Full summit briefing and gear check from your guide
Early dinner at 5:30 PM · Sleep at 7:00 PM · Wake call at midnight
Breakfast Lunch Early Dinner (5:30 PM)

Midnight wake-up. Thermos of hot tea, layers on, headtorches adjusted. Your guide leads you into the African night. The ascent to Stella Point (5,756m) on the crater rim takes approximately 6–7 hours of steady, relentless climbing through scree and volcanic rock. The final push to Uhuru Peak is another 45–60 minutes along the crater rim.

At 5,895m — the highest point on the African continent — the world stops. Glaciers, clouds below, the curvature of the Earth. Your guide will take your summit photo at the famous wooden sign. Take a moment. You've earned it.

Descent via the Mweka Route back to Mweka Camp takes approximately 5–6 hours on loose volcanic scree. A late lunch is served at high camp before the final descent.

00:00 — Wake call, hot drinks, summit departure
~06:00 — Reach Stella Point (5,756m) on the crater rim
~07:00 — Summit at Uhuru Peak (5,895m) — certificate awarded
Descent via Mweka Route — 5–6 hours to Mweka Camp
Late lunch and overnight at Mweka Camp (3,100m)
Summit snacks & thermos Late Lunch (descent) Celebration Dinner

After a well-deserved sleep, you wake to birdsong in the rainforest and begin the final 3–4 hour descent to Mweka Gate at 1,640m. The trail winds back down through the lush montane forest — a beautiful, gentle end to an extraordinary journey.

At the gate, you sign out, your guide presents you with your official summit certificate, and the team celebrations begin. Tip distribution for porters and crew follows — a meaningful moment acknowledging everyone who helped you reach the top.

Leisurely descent through rainforest to Mweka Gate (1,640m)
Sign-out procedures and official summit certificate presentation
Porter tip distribution and farewell celebrations

Breakfast Lunch (Moshi)

A free morning in Moshi — enjoy breakfast, explore the local market, or simply rest your legs. Our team arranges airport transfer to Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO) based on your flight time.

If you're extending your trip to the Serengeti, Zanzibar or a Kenya safari, our team coordinates all onward arrangements. We'll see you off and hope to welcome you back to East Africa again soon. Asante sana — thank you so very much!

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

No technical climbing experience is required. Kilimanjaro is a high-altitude trek, not a technical climb. You need good physical fitness and determination — not ropes, harnesses or crampons. Our guides lead groups of all fitness levels safely to the summit every week.
You should be able to walk 5–8 hours consecutively with a daypack. Regular hiking, running or cycling for 2–3 months before your trek is ideal preparation. The Machame Route is rated "moderate" — no one needs to be an elite athlete, but you shouldn't be starting from scratch fitness-wise either.
Altitude sickness (Acute Mountain Sickness) is caused by ascending too quickly and not allowing the body to adjust to lower oxygen levels. Symptoms include headache, nausea and fatigue. We prevent it through our "walk high, sleep low" profile, twice-daily pulse oximeter monitoring, our pacing strategy, and by carrying supplemental oxygen. Diamox (acetazolamide) can also be taken as a preventative — consult your doctor before travel.
All our treks are private — we never mix your group with strangers. Whether you're solo, a couple, a family or a group of 12, the mountain team is dedicated entirely to your group. This means you walk at your pace, stop when you want to, and receive personalised attention from your guide.
Yes — we can arrange rental of sleeping bags (−15°C rated), trekking poles, gaiters and other gear in Moshi at reasonable rates. Please let us know at least 2 weeks in advance so we can confirm availability. We recommend bringing your own trekking boots that are properly broken in.
Cancellations made 90+ days before departure receive a full deposit refund. Between 60–90 days, 50% of the deposit is retained. Within 60 days of departure, the full deposit is non-refundable due to park fee commitments. We strongly recommend purchasing comprehensive travel insurance that covers trek cancellation.
