About This Tour

Tanzania's Greatest Safari Loop

The Northern Circuit is Tanzania's signature wildlife experience — a seamless private journey through four of East Africa's most iconic ecosystems, led by guides who have spent their entire lives in these parks.

Beginning in Arusha, the circuit weaves south through Tarangire National Park's ancient elephant herds, across the flamingo-pink shores of Lake Manyara, down into the extraordinary caldera of Ngorongoro Crater, and finally out onto the infinite plains of the Serengeti — where a million wildebeest share the savanna with all of the Big Five.

Every departure is private. Your vehicle, your guide, your pace. You stop when something captivates you, stay as long as you wish beside a pride of lions, and adjust the programme daily based on what the bush is delivering. This is not a group tour — it is your personal safari, designed around you.

Day-by-Day Programme

7-Day Northern Circuit Itinerary

Shown as 7 days — fully customisable from 5 to 8 days. Ask us to shorten, extend or adjust any section.

Your driver-guide meets you at Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO) holding a Big 5 Africa Safari sign. Transfer to your Arusha hotel — typically a 45-minute drive depending on traffic. The afternoon is free to rest, explore Arusha town, or visit the Arusha Cultural Heritage Centre.

In the evening your lead guide visits for a full safari briefing: the route, the parks, what to expect, packing tips and an introduction to the Maasai culture you'll encounter. This is a chance to ask every question you have before the adventure begins tomorrow.

Airport transfer in private 4WD to Arusha hotel
Afternoon at leisure — town walk or shopping optional
Evening safari briefing with your lead guide
Welcome dinner at the hotel
Dinner included

After an early breakfast, depart Arusha for Tarangire National Park — a 2-hour drive southeast. Enter the park in time for the morning game drive, immediately encountering Tarangire's defining feature: elephants. Hundreds of them. During the dry season (June–October) entire herds of 200–300 elephants congregate along the Tarangire River, the only water source for miles in every direction.

Tarangire is also remarkable for its ancient baobab trees — some over 1,000 years old — and for its exceptional bird life with over 550 species recorded. Afternoon and evening game drives continue before settling into your tented camp for sundowners and dinner under the stars.

Morning departure from Arusha after breakfast
Full-day game drives — elephants, baobabs, lions, leopards
Bush picnic lunch served in the park
Evening sundowner drinks at scenic viewpoint
Breakfast Bush Picnic Lunch Dinner

Drive north to Lake Manyara National Park — a compact but extraordinarily diverse park tucked between the walls of the Great Rift Valley escarpment and the alkaline soda lake that gives it its name. Lake Manyara is famous for its tree-climbing lions, a rare population that has developed the unusual habit of resting in the branches of fever trees above the ground.

The lake itself is often tinged pink by hundreds of thousands of flamingos, while hippo pools in the park's freshwater streams produce some of the most photographed wildlife scenes in East Africa. An optional Maasai village visit can be arranged this afternoon before settling in at your lodge above the escarpment with panoramic rift valley views.

Morning departure for Lake Manyara (~45 min drive)
Full game drive — tree-climbing lions, flamingos, hippo pools

Today is arguably the centrepiece of the entire Northern Circuit. Ngorongoro Crater is the world's largest intact volcanic caldera — a 260 km² natural enclosure that contains approximately 25,000 large mammals in a single self-contained ecosystem. It is the only place in Africa where you are almost guaranteed to see all of the Big Five in a single day, including the critically endangered black rhino.

You descend 600m on a dirt track from the crater rim into a landscape that feels utterly otherworldly — open grassland, soda lake, hippo pools and acacia woodland, all contained within perfect walls of ancient volcanic rock. Your guide navigates to each of the crater's wildlife hotspots systematically, ensuring you spend quality time with lions, elephants, rhino, hippos and the extraordinary birdlife.

Pre-dawn crater rim breakfast with panoramic views
Full-day descent into the crater floor (600m down)
Black rhino tracking with your expert guide

The drive from Ngorongoro to the central Serengeti passes through the Ngorongoro Conservation Area — itself teeming with wildlife — before entering the Serengeti proper through the Naabi Hill Gate. The drive itself is a game drive, and your guide makes multiple stops along the way.

The Central Serengeti (Seronera Valley) is one of the most reliable areas for leopard sightings in all of Africa. A network of lugga (seasonal watercourses) lined with yellow-barked fever trees provides perfect leopard habitat, and your guide knows exactly where to look. Lions are resident year-round, and cheetah frequent the open plains around Seronera.

Morning departure from Ngorongoro highland camp
Game drives en route through the NCA buffer zone
Enter the Serengeti via Naabi Hill Gate

All Meals

A full day in the Serengeti — one of the great wildlife experiences on the planet. Your guide departs camp before first light, positioning for the magic of the African dawn. Predators are still active; a pride of lions may be finishing a night-time hunt, a cheetah scanning for impala on the open plain.

Depending on the season, your guide navigates to the area of the Serengeti with the highest current wildlife concentrations. From December to July, vast wildebeest herds blanket the southern and eastern plains. From July to October, the focus shifts north toward the Mara River crossing zones. There is no bad time in the Serengeti — only different spectacles.

Pre-dawn departure — predator window at sunrise
Full-day drives across multiple Serengeti ecosystems
Bush picnic lunch in the field
Afternoon drives through golden hour to sundowner spot

One final morning game drive before departing the Serengeti. Depending on your flight time, your guide will plan the return drive to Arusha accordingly — typically a 5–6 hour journey with a lunch stop en route. Guests with afternoon or evening flights can linger in the park until midday; early-morning flights may require an overnight near Arusha on night six instead.

Airport transfer to Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO) or Julius Nyerere Airport (DAR) as required, in time for your onward flight. Your Big 5 Africa Safari experience concludes — but the memories begin now.

Frequently Asked Questions

The dry season from June to October is considered peak season — excellent wildlife viewing, dusty but navigable tracks, and the highest density of animals concentrated around water sources. January–February is the calving season on the Serengeti plains — spectacular for predator action. The long rains in April–May make some tracks challenging but also deliver lush green scenery and far fewer tourists.
We recommend booking 3–6 months in advance for peak season (June–October) to secure your preferred lodges and guide availability. Outside peak season, 4–8 weeks is usually sufficient.
